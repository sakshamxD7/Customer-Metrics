RFM Segmentation Framework

What is RFM? RFM is a behavior-based database segmentation framework that ranks customers based on transaction history:

  • Recency (R): Days elapsed between the customer's last invoice and the final dataset date (2026-06-09).
  • Frequency (F): The count of unique invoice orders placed by the customer during the 9-day period.
  • Monetary (M): The sum of all net transaction amounts associated with the customer's orders.

Cohort Classification: R scores are mapped directly from inactivity windows (0 days = 5 to 4+ days = 1). F and M scores use rank percentiles to generate five equal-sized bins. Spatially, these scores compile into:

  • Champions: High score in all three indicators (value leaders).
  • Loyal Customers: Regular transactions and consistent spend.
  • At Risk / Need Attention: Inactive for 3+ days, but with strong purchase history.

Recency Calculation Note

Due to the ultra-dense 9-day transaction timeline (2026-06-01 to 2026-06-09), recency is calculated directly against the final timeline date of 2026-06-09. Scores are mapped: 0 days = 5, 1 day = 4, 2 days = 3, 3 days = 2, and 4+ days = 1.
